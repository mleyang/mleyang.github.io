Projects

太空狐狸 SpaceFox Unity VR Project (2015)

During my final quarter at UCLA I made a space exploration game featuring a fox character which I designed and made. Given my strong interest and curiosity for VR, I created a world in Unity and configured it for the Oculus to be explored in first person perspective. The player can explore the planet while collecting different gemstones. I chose a low-poly aesthetic as a style choice as well as to help with optimization for real-time rendering.

Reel Breakdown

1 Slight Night Shiver (2014)

Programs used: Maya, Mental Ray, RealFlow, Adobe Photoshop, Nuke, Adobe After Effects

I am responsible for all aspects of this project. I wanted to raise awareness for marine life by creating a certain mood for my audience. I painted the texture of the whale in Photoshop. The animation for the jellyfish was done using expressions, and the transparency was achieved by using the sampler info node. I had difficulty getting caustics to look natural being projected onto my whale, but I resolved this by generating caustics using noise patterns in Nuke and connecting the image sequence to the color attribute in the lights in Maya.

2 Forever Alloon (2013)

Programs used: Maya, Mental Ray, Nuke, After Effects, Zbrush

This is my first attempt at making a 3D short after self-teaching Maya. It is a self-initiated short film that I wrote, directed, and created in my own time. The story is about a balloon that is not as brightly colored as the others but longs for a human companion. The main balloon character and its expressions were modeled using Zbrush then imported into Maya. Everything else was modeled in Maya. The string simulation was done using Maya’s nCloth and the grass was done with fur simulation and shaded with green tones.

3 All Men Are Flowers (2013)

Programs used: Nuke

I was responsible for all the VFX for a UCLA Film School grad thesis film. This is one of the shots I worked on. I edited ink drop footages and inserted a light source behind them to create the growing galaxy. Some of the floating particles were generated using Nuke particles.
